Key Events, Terms & Abbreviations
Couture Las Vegas — Top US luxury jewellery trade show, held each June in Las Vegas. Primary first store partnership entry point for the USA market — trade show contacts become boutiques and jewellers that stock Henig product.
JA New York — Jewelers of America trade show, held each August in New York. Covers 80%+ of US luxury buyers.
Inhorgenta Munich — Germany's leading jewellery trade show, held each February. Non-negotiable entry point for the German-speaking luxury market.
JCK Toronto — Canada's leading jewellery trade show, held each June. Primary store partnership entry point for the Canadian market — boutique contacts become stores that stock Henig product.
Paris Fashion Week (PFW) — Held each January and September. Key brand visibility and press networking event for the French luxury market.
GIA / IGI — Gemological Institute of America / International Gemological Institute. The two most trusted diamond grading certifications globally.
RJC — Responsible Jewellery Council. Industry body whose certification signals ethical sourcing — critical for the German and EU market.
Kimberley Process (KP) — International certification scheme that confirms diamonds are conflict-free. Mandatory trust signal in all markets.
ESG — Environmental, Social and Governance. Sustainability and ethical sourcing credentials — especially powerful in Germany, France, and Ireland.
Lab-Grown Diamonds (LGD) — Diamonds created in a laboratory, chemically identical to mined diamonds. ~50% penetration in Canada and growing in all markets.
Goldschmiede — German independent master jewellers. Hold approximately 70% of luxury jewellery sales in Germany. The primary in-store channel for the German market.
Xiaohongshu (XHS) — Chinese social commerce app (also called Little Red Book). Primary platform for luxury discovery in China.
CPM — Cost per 1,000 impressions. A standard advertising pricing metric. USA: $25–$35 · Germany/France: $15–$25 · Ireland: lower.
CPC — Cost per click. Used mainly for Pinterest and Google Shopping. Canada/Australia: $1.50–$3.00.
ASP — Average Selling Price. USA: $3,500 · Germany: $2,800 · France: $2,900 · Canada: $2,700 · Ireland: $2,800.
ROI — Return on Investment. Phase success targets are based on achieving at least 60% of the model ROI midpoint in Year 1.
VR Try-On (Zakeke / GlamAR) — Virtual try-on feature for henigdiamonds.com. Customers use their phone camera to see a ring on their hand — no app download needed. Two platform options: Zakeke (3D visualisation + customisation, good for showing variants) or GlamAR (augmented reality, specialised for rings and necklaces). USA-only add-on. Conservative = Scenario B (build starts Phase 2); Moderate/Aggressive = Scenario A (full Year 1 build, live before holiday peak).
Email & SMS Marketing — Automated sequences for gift guides, cart abandonment reminders, and post-purchase follow-ups. Sent directly to customers who sign up on henigdiamonds.com.
